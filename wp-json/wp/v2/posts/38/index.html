If you’re reading this you’re probably planning your trip around Thailand and more specifically to beautiful Koh Samui. On this post, we explain how to get to Bangkok from Koh Samui. Also, if you are interested in the other way around, Koh Samui to Bangkok, the same rules and options apply. These are the 4 options:<\/p>\n

Flight to Bangkok from Koh Samui<\/strong><\/a><\/h2>\n

The most comfortable way but only for those travelling on\u00a0a deep budget. Bangkok Airways runs this route many times during the day at around 130$ one way. The flight is 1hour 15 minutes and Bangkok Airways is spot on, including their tasty snack. Book here in SkyScanner<\/a><\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

Ferry + Bus<\/strong><\/a><\/h2>\n

This option will take you to Bangkok in 12-13 hours depending on the starting port and the company. We have NOT done it but we’ve heard some opinions. It’s not\u00a0too<\/em> bad but keeps in mind you will be on a boat for a long time and then a long run in a bus will be waiting for you. Not much fun, especially on choppy days.<\/p>\n

The price is around 1200\u0e3f-1400\u0e3f (38-44$). Book here<\/strong><\/a><\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

Ferry + Van + Train<\/strong><\/a><\/h2>\n

A bit more comfortable but very long, around 18 hours. A ferry will take you to the mainland and then by van, you will get to Suratthani train station. Then you need to take a train to Bangkok. The train goes from 18:37 to 06:30 so you can get some good sleep in the night.<\/p>\n

The price goes from 2289\u0e3f for a train first class ticket (72$) or 1758\u0e3f for a second class ticket (55$). Book here<\/strong><\/a><\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

Ferry + Van + Flight<\/strong><\/a><\/h3>\n

Our favourite, since it is good value on money, comfort and time. You could do the whole trip in around 6 hours depending on how long you need to wait for your flight. You’ll be taken from\u00a0Na Thon\u00a0<\/strong>port to mainland in a ferry run by Seatran Ferry<\/em>. The ride is actually nice, safe boat and it has a bar where you can get some instant soups at a good price. After an hour and a half, you get to the mainland, where a van is waiting for you to take you to the airport. Once there you just need to take a flight to Bangkok.<\/p>\n

There are very economics flights provided by Thai Smile (great) or Thai Lion (just ok). The price for the boat+van goes from 230\u0e3f for the very early boats to 400\u0e3f, plus 30-40$ for the flight. In total, you will get spend around 40-50$, almost as cheap as the Ferry + Bus option but way more comfortable and fast.\u00a0Book here<\/strong><\/a><\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

The last tip<\/strong>: make sure you find a proper way to get to the port. We used an app called NavigoApp (Uber\/Grab style) because we were told it was way cheaper than normal taxis in Koh Samui. We paid 550\u0e3f from Lamai Beach to Na Thon. However, some fellow travellers did the same trip using a regular taxi for 350\u0e3f. I think they just got lucky with the taxi driver, but overall our experience with NavigoApp was awful as the app barely work.<\/p>\n
