Planning your travel from Koh Samui to Bangkok? We’ve got you covered! Here are four options to consider, each with its own unique blend of comfort, cost, and travel time.<\/p>\n

Flight to Bangkok from Koh Samui<\/strong><\/a><\/h2>\n

The quickest but most expensive option is a direct flight. Bangkok Airways operates multiple flights daily, with a one-way ticket costing around $130. The flight duration is a mere 1 hour and 15 minutes, and the airline’s service, including their tasty snack, is commendable.<\/p>\n

Ferry + Bus<\/strong><\/a><\/h2>\n

This option will transport you to Bangkok in approximately 12-13 hours, depending on the starting port and the company. While this is not the most comfortable option, especially on choppy days, it’s a viable choice for those on a tight budget. The cost<\/a> is around 1200\u0e3f-1400\u0e3f ($38-$44).<\/p>\n

Ferry + Van + Train<\/strong><\/a><\/h2>\n

This option is a bit more comfortable but also longer, taking around 18 hours. A ferry will transport you to the mainland, and then a van will take you to the Suratthani train station. From there, a train will take you to Bangkok. The train departs at 18:37 and arrives at 06:30, allowing you to get some sleep during the journey. The price ranges from 2289\u0e3f for a first-class train ticket ($72) to 1758\u0e3f for a second-class ticket ($55)<\/p>\n

The price<\/a> goes from 2289\u0e3f for a train first-class ticket (72$) or 1758\u0e3f for a second-class ticket (55$).<\/p>\n

Ferry + Van + Flight<\/strong><\/a><\/h3>\n

This is our preferred option, as it offers a good balance of cost, comfort, and time. The entire trip can be completed in around 6 hours, depending on your flight’s waiting time. A Seatran Ferry will transport you from Na Thon port to the mainland, followed by a van ride to the airport. From there, you can catch a flight to Bangkok. Thai Smile and Thai Lion offer economical flights. The price for the boat and van ranges from 230\u0e3f for the early boats to 400\u0e3f, plus $30-$40 for the flight. In total, you’ll spend around $40-$50, making this option almost as cheap as the Ferry + Bus option but significantly more comfortable and faster.<\/p>\n

Happy travelling!<\/p>\n
