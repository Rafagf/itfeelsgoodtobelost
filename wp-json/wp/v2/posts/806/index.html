“Updated June 2023:<\/strong> This post has been updated to reflect the current information and tips for your 3 week Vietnam itinerary. Enjoy planning your adventure!”<\/em><\/p>\n

Planning a trip to Vietnam is an adventure filled with excitement. However, with Vietnam’s vast landscapes and rich culture, it can be challenging to decide how long to spend in each place to truly experience its charm. The good news for you is that we’re here to help you organise your 3 week\u00a0Vietnam itinerary.<\/strong><\/p>\n

With our guide, you’ll go from north to south, but you could start in the south if it works best for you, it’s not a problem at all. Worth mentioning, we take our time when visiting places, as we don’t like visiting places just to put a tick on it.<\/p>\n

How much money do I need?<\/h2>\n

The currency is the Vietnamese Dong. You’ll get about 23,000 Dongs for a Dollar.<\/p>\n

1 USD = 23,000 VND<\/p>\n

Vietnam is a very cheap country, but it can also become expensive if you aren’t careful. Budget accommodations range between 15$ to 30$ per night, while food ranges between 2$ and 7$. If you really take good care of your money, you can allocate a 30$ budget per day. A 50$ per day budget will make a great holiday though.<\/p>\n

  • 15$ – 30$ per night<\/li>\n
  • 2$ – 7$<\/li>\n
  • ~1$<\/li>\n
  • TOTAL: 30$ per day<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    Visa<\/span><\/h2>\n

    A very important thing to double-check. Vietnam is more restrictive than other countries in South East Asia. Many nationalities get a 15 days visa on arrival, but others will need to apply for it before landing in Vietnam.<\/p>\n

    If for example, you want to follow our 3 week Vietnam itinerary, you’ll need to apply for a visa.\u00a0You can do it online, taking 3 days and costing you 30$.\u00a0If you didn’t do your homework and you’re short on time, there are some companies who will make a visa for you in even 15 minutes, but prepare the wallet…<\/p>\n

    3 week Vietnam Itinerary<\/h2>\n

    <\/p>\n

    Hanoi – 3 nights<\/h2>\n

    Hanoi, the vibrant capital of Vietnam, is the first stop on our 3-week Vietnam itinerary. While it’s possible to explore the city’s highlights in 2 days, taking it easy can offer a deeper appreciation of Hanoi’s bustling life.<\/p>\n

    The capital of Vietnam is one of the most lively cities we’ve ever been to. People just pass their day sitting on the street and sipping (amazing!) coffee. You will see the worst traffic you’ve ever witnessed, but don’t let that put you off. Hanoi is a beautiful city full of lovely quarters, things to do and new food to experience.<\/p>\n

    For a detailed guide to Hanoi – check our\u00a0post<\/a><\/p>\n

    Must do things<\/span><\/h3>\n

    <\/p>\n

      \n
    • Roam around Hoan Kiem lake. Visit Ngoc Son, a small temple situated in the middle of the lake.<\/li>\n
    • Wander around the Old and French Quarters.<\/li>\n
    • Visit the Ho Chi Minh mausoleum. It’s incredible how much respect North-Vietnamese’s have for their old leader.<\/li>\n
    • Visit Hoa Lo prison, colloquially known as “Hanoi Hilton”.<\/li>\n
    • See the train passing on the\u00a0 famous “Train Street”<\/b><\/li>\n
    • We did a free food tour of Hanoi Free Walking Tours<\/a> and it was amazing. The guides are students and they will take you to places that you would not go on your own. Great way to get you started with Vietnamese food.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

      <\/p>\n

      Other things you could do…<\/span><\/h3>\n
        \n
      • Don Xuan Market. You can find pretty much anything here.<\/li>\n
      • Museum of Anthropology, especially interesting if you’re into finding out about the different tribes that live in the North of Vietnam.<\/li>\n
      • Water puppet theatre.<\/li>\n
      • Opera house.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

        TIP:\u00a0<\/strong>If you get to Hanoi via plane, don’t take a creepy taxi to go to the city centre. Download GRAB. It cost us 10$ to go to the city centre, 3 times cheaper than the price the taxi drivers offer.<\/p>\n

        Sapa -2 nights<\/span><\/h2>\n

        The landscapes of the very north of Vietnam are a must-see and you shouldn’t miss them. The easiest place to visit is Sapa, this comes at a cost though. We’ve heard Sapa can be very touristic and a bit fake. However, if you’re clever and book the right trekking, your experience will have nothing to do with that.<\/p>\n

        TIP<\/strong>: You can avoid paying for one night of accommodation by taking this<\/a><\/strong> very comfortable night bus.<\/p>\n

        Our Trekking<\/span><\/h3>\n

        We booked a trek called “Off the beaten track” with Sapa O’Chau<\/a>\u00a0and it was one of the best experiences we’ve had when travelling. More about this\u00a0here<\/a>\u00a0and\u00a0here<\/a>.<\/p>\n

        Where to stay<\/span><\/h3>\n

        Budget option: Sapa O\u2019Chau<\/a><\/strong><\/p>\n

        Sapa O’Chau is also a humble hotel. You can stay here the night before\/after your trekking. The rooms are clean and quite cheap, so it has good value.<\/p>\n

        Ha Long Bay – 1 or 2 nights<\/span><\/h2>\n

        Our 3 week Vietnam itinerary would not be complete without a visit to Ha Long Bay, one of the highlights of every visitor to Vietnam. A boat cruise will take you around the Bay for 1 or 2 nights, depending on how long you choose to stay. We’re budget travellers, so we chose 1 night. Most of the companies will include transportation from Hanoi to Ha Long Bay in the price.<\/p>\n

        We’ve written this post<\/a> about how to choose between Sapa or Ha Long Bay in case you’re short on time or budget.<\/p>\n

        TIP<\/strong>: Boats usually spend the night in Ha Long Bay, meaning you end up sharing the night with hundreds of boats. Some cruises take a slightly different route, sleeping in Bai Tu Long Bay. This will make your experience MUCH BETTER. Our sunset was unforgettable.<\/p>\n

        <\/p>\n

        Our choice<\/span><\/h3>\n

        We took the package called “Off the beaten track” offered by Indochina Junk<\/a>. With this option, you’ll spend the night in Bai Tu Long Bay.<\/p>\n

        Ninh Binh -2 nights<\/span><\/h2>\n

        We got to Ninh Binh via train from Hanoi. We had a great time biking around Tam Coc, although we were a bit unlucky as we had heavy rain for one day, meaning we couldn’t make it to the national park. Still, we had an amazing time in this beautiful countryside area.<\/p>\n

        Must do things<\/span><\/h3>\n

        <\/p>\n

          \n
        • Tam Coc Lake tour. One hour stroll in a small boat. The landscape is very similar to Ha Long Bay but it is still worth it.<\/li>\n
        • Bich Dong Pagoda<\/li>\n
        • Bird Valley<\/li>\n
        • Mua cave. The cave is the least important thing, make sure you hike the mountain to get the perfect views!<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

          <\/p>\n

          TIP<\/strong>: Rent a bike (most hostels provide very cheap bikes) and cycle everywhere. The roads are very safe and the views deserve to be appreciated. You won’t get the same experience using Tuk Tuks or motorbikes.<\/p>\n

          <\/p>\n

          <\/h3>\n

          Where to stay<\/span><\/h3>\n

          Comfort option<\/strong>:\u00a0Tam Coc Horizon Bungalow<\/a><\/strong><\/p>\n

          Outstanding bungalows surrounded by amazing landscapes. The atmosphere and the staff are both simply incredible. Probably the best place to stay in Ninh Binh.<\/p>\n

          <\/p>\n

          Hue – 1 night<\/span><\/h2>\n

          We got to Hue by night bus from Ninh Binh. Be aware the bus wasn’t particularly safe, nothing to do with the one we took to go to Sapa.<\/p>\n

          Hue is the city of the Citadel. While the Citadel is impressive and very interesting from a historical point of view, if we had to cut down one day from our itinerary it would be Hue. Still, we had a nice day and enjoyed the city. During the night there is a nice market by the river and many nice options for dinner.<\/p>\n

          <\/p>\n

          Must do things<\/span><\/h3>\n
            \n
          • Hue Citadel and Forbidden City<\/li>\n
          • Walk stroll next to the river. There are many markets and restaurants<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

            Where to stay<\/span><\/h3>\n

            Budget option:\u00a0Bonjour Hostel<\/a><\/strong><\/p>\n

            Well located, very clean and offers rooms under 10$ the night depending on the dates. Delicious breakfast included! Just has tremendous value.<\/p>\n

            Comfort option:\u00a0The Scarlett Boutique Hotel<\/a><\/strong><\/p>\n

            Very affordable luxury rooms (about 50$ the night). One of the most visited and prestigious hotels in Hue, you can’t go wrong with it.<\/p>\n

            <\/p>\n

            Hoi An -6 nights<\/span><\/h2>\n

            <\/p>\n

            Continuing our 3 week Vietnam itinerary, we’ll get to Hoi An. To get to this gem town, we strongly recommend the train if you come from Hue. It goes next to the Hai Van Pass, a 21 km mountain pass with beautiful views of the sea.<\/p>\n

            Without a doubt, Hoi An is our favourite city in Vietnam. With its pristine beaches, lush rice fields, and a beautiful old town illuminated by lanterns, Hoi An offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage that we found truly captivating.<\/p>\n

            Must do things<\/span><\/h3>\n
              \n
            • An Bang beach. Relaxing beach with very good and tasty restaurants. Also, you can do parasailing for 30$ 2 people. We did it and was AMAZING.<\/li>\n
            • The old town at night. Have a stroll in the old town at night, when all the lanterns are on. Simply beautiful.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

              <\/p>\n

                \n
              • No better city to buy souvenirs. We bought a few too many lanterns (but we don’t regret it!).<\/li>\n
              • Ride a motorbike around Hoi An. We’ll never forget the joy of riding the motorbike from the city centre to the beach, passing next to the rice fields.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

                <\/p>\n

                Other things you could do<\/span><\/h3>\n
                  \n
                • Ancient town tour. Old houses, museums, Japanese covered bridge, etc.<\/li>\n
                • Marble Mountains, halfway between Da Nang and Hoi An.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

                  TIP<\/strong>: Motorbike is a must in Hoi An. The roads are good and traffic is more “western” than in other places of the country. Another plus: you won’t have to deal with tuk-tuk or taxi drivers.<\/p>\n

                  <\/p>\n

                  Modern construction, clean, fresh and very comfortable. The staff is amazing and will help you with anything you need. Bicycles and scooters are available for rental at very affordable prices. Amazing value (rooms under 15$ the night).<\/p>\n

                  Comfort option:\u00a0Little Town Villa<\/a><\/strong><\/p>\n

                  Luxury but an affordable villa in the old town. Spacious rooms and tasteful decoration. The breakfast is great, with both local and international options. Swimming pool to cool down after a long walk around the old town.<\/p>\n

                  Da Lat -3 nights<\/span><\/h2>\n

                  As we continue our 3-week Vietnam itinerary, it’s time for an exhilarating adventure! Da Lat, renowned for its rolling hills, verdant forests, and stunning waterfalls, is an unmissable destination for nature lovers.”<\/p>\n

                    \n

                  • Visiting Pongour Falls<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

                    Ho Chi Minh -2 nights<\/span><\/h2>\n

                    <\/p>\n

                    The last stop of our 3 week Vietnam itinerary is Ho Chi Minh, also known as Saigon. We stayed 2 nights but 1 night-2 days works well too.<\/p>\n

                    The capital of what it was “South Vietnam”, it is as vibrant or more than Hanoi. Also, it is a great city to learn more about Vietnam’s war with the Americans.<\/p>\n
