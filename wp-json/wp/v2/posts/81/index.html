There is this false belief, that you must be rich in order to travel. When explaining to some acquaintances how long we were gonna travel for, some of them would react like “Wow, you guys are loaded!<\/em>“. We are far from being loaded! The funny thing is, those who say that probably go on holidays for 2 weeks, spending the same money than us in 2 months! That’s why we want to share our 3 rules to travel cheap!<\/p>\n

We’ve discovered that spending more money usually doesn’t make your trip any better, in fact, if anything, it makes it less authentic.<\/strong><\/p>\n

“I only have 2 weeks of holidays per year. I an definitively staying in that 5 stars hotel, look at that bath tube!”<\/em><\/p><\/blockquote>\n

1. Choose your destination wisely<\/strong><\/h2>\n

Let’s be honest, your holidays are going to depend a lot on the country you are at. It’s not the same to visit Myanmar than visiting Switzerland. Whenever you go somewhere expensive, be aware that there is a tradeoff…<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Moving by taxi if you feel tired vs public transport, meaning you end up exhausted every day<\/li>\n
  • Eating wherever you feel into vs. eating in McDonald’s because you can’t afford anything else (e.g. Hong Kong is famous for its super cheap Michelin Star restaurants!)<\/li>\n
  • Boat to that magic cave + kayaking + cooking lesson + motorbike tour vs free walking tour around the city<\/li>\n
  • You get the idea…<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
