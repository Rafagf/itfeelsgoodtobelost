During our visit to Ho Chi Minh (also called Saigon), we happened to witness the strangest protest ever!<\/p>\n

Hundreds of people riding motorbikes <\/strong>gathered in the city center (District 1) to protest in front of a government building. I don’t think we’ve ever seen so many flags and motorbikes in such a small space! We found interesting the police’s behavior; they soon arrived to shut down the protest, but it was clear they were in favor of it. They took their time before forcing riders to move away from the building. After this, they kept riding the city center for quite a while.<\/p>\n

By the looks of it, we could tell was something against immigrants but we didn’t feel threatened at any moment, meaning it wasn’t related to tourists. After a while, a very sophisticated Vietnamese man with a very correct English explained to us what was this about. Turns out there is a law in Vietnam that allows foreigners to rent properties for 99 years. Vietnamese feel Chinese are taking advantage of this law and they are occupying big chunks of towns, making them more Chinese than Vietnamese. From what we saw there, Vietnamese are not a fan of their Chinese neighbors, as you could read big banners saying “Chinese out of Vietnam NOW” or “Vietnam for Vietnamese”. <\/span><\/p>\n

They need to expand its economic relationships with China, it’s neighbor to the north, but at the same time their population feels they’re selling their country for money.<\/p>\n
