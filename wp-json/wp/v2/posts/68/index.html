Embarking on an adventure exploring Koh Samet, a lovely island nestled in the Gulf of Thailand, is a perfect escape from the bustling city of Bangkok. This comprehensive travel guide will help you navigate through the island’s pristine beaches, amazing sunsets, and vibrant nightlife. Koh Samet is still a hidden gem, so make sure you explore this paradise before it becomes another crowded island!<\/p>\n

If you are like us and made the mistake to spend some days in Pattaya you’ll find exploring Koh Samet a refreshing experience.<\/p>\n

How to get there?<\/strong><\/h3>\n

We went to Koh Samet from Pattaya. If you’re going from Bangkok, I’d expect there are buses from Ekkamai bus station to Ban Phe port. Worst scenario, you could easily go to Pattaya and then go to Ban Phe. This is how we did it from Pattaya:<\/p>\n

    \n
  1. Go to this location<\/a><\/strong>. In front of the Tesco Lotus, there is a small travel office that organizes trips by van.<\/b><\/li>\n
  2. Tell them you want to go to Ban-Phe port<\/strong> to then go to Koh Samet.<\/b> The trip takes an hour and it costs 300 bahts (2 people).<\/li>\n
  3. Once in the port, walk to the ticket office. There are slow and fast boats. We took a fast boat, which after haggling a bit, cost us 250 bahts (2 people).<\/li>\n
  4. A short 15-minute ride later, you’ll arrive at Koh Samet. A coast guard will approach you to collect the national park fee, which is 200 baht. Rest assured, exploring Koh Samet is worth every penny!<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n

    <\/p>\n

    How to move around<\/strong><\/h3>\n

    The island is fairly small, but you’ll need transport to move around. Options are:<\/p>\n

      \n
    • Hopping in big taxis that move around the island all day.<\/strong> The price depends on where you’re going but keep in mind they’re generally pricey.<\/li>\n
    • Rent a bike and cycle around!<\/strong> Ecological and cheap.<\/li>\n
    • Our favorite: hire a motorbike <\/strong>(250 bahts per day). Only for those who know how to drive. If you’re a beginner, you should be fine as it is quite easy to drive in Koh Samet. Drive safely!<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

      A few things to convince you why exploring Koh Samet is worth it<\/strong><\/h2>\n

      Hat Sai Kaew Beach<\/strong><\/h3>\n

      The most popular beach. During the day expect a lot of people, towels, activities like snorkeling and so on.  In the night the atmosphere is stunning: nice restaurants, fire shows, relaxed parties, locals hanging out…<\/p>\n
