Planning a trip to Vietnam is definitely exciting. However, Vietnam is quite a big country and is hard to gauge how long you should spend in each place to make the most of it. The good news for you is that we’re here to help you organise your 3 week Vietnam itinerary. With our guide, you’ll go from north to south, but you could start in the south if it works best for you, it’s not a problem at all. Worth mentioning, we take our time when visiting places, as we don’t like visiting places just to put a tick on it.

But before we start…

HOW MUCH MONEY DO I NEED?

The currency is the Vietnamese Dong. You’ll get about 23,000 Dongs for a Dollar.

1 USD = 23,000 VND 

Vietnam is a very cheap country, but it can also become expensive if you aren’t careful. Budget accommodations range between 15$ to 30$ per night, while food ranges between 2$ and 7$. If you really take good care of your money, you can allocate a 30$ budget per day. A 50$ per day budget will make a great holiday though.

  • 15$ - 30$ per night
  • 2$ - 7$
  • ~1$
  • TOTAL: 30$ per day

VISA

A very important thing to double check. Vietnam is more restrictive than other countries in South East Asia. Many nationalities get a 15 days visa on arrival, but others will need to apply for it before landing in Vietnam.

If for example, you want to follow our 3 week Vietnam itinerary, you’ll need to apply for a visa. You can do it online, taking 3 days and costing you 30$. If you didn’t do your homework and you’re short on time, there are some companies who will make a visa for you in even 15 minutes, but prepare the wallet…

3 WEEK VIETNAM ITINERARY

Hanoi- 3 nights

Hanoi is the first stop of our 3 week Vietnam itinerary. You could easily see everything in 2 days but we think you will appreciate Hanoi more if you take it easy.

The capital of Vietnam is one of the most lively cities we’ve ever been to. People just pass their day sitting on the street and sipping (amazing!) coffee. You will see the worst traffic you’ve ever witnessed, but don’t let that put you off. Hanoi is a beautiful city full of lovely quarters, things to do and new food to experience.

For a detailed guide to Hanoi – check our post 

Must do things…

– Roam around Hoan Kiem lake. Visit Ngoc Son, a small temple situated in the middle of the lake.
– Wander around the Old and French Quarters.
– Visit the Ho Chi Minh mausoleum. It’s incredible how much respect North-Vietnamese’s have for their old leader.
– Visit Hoa Lo prison, colloquially known as “Hanoi Hilton”.
– See the train passing on the  famous “Train street”
– We did a free food tour of https://www.hanoifreewalkingtours.com/ and it was amazing. The guides are students and they will take you to places that you would not go on your own. Great way to get you started with Vietnamese’s food.

Other things you could do…

– Don Xuan Market. You can find pretty much anything here.
– Museum of Anthropology, especially interesting if you’re into finding out about the different tribes that live in the North of Vietnam.
– Water puppet theatre.
– Opera house.

TIP: If you get to Hanoi via plane, don’t take a creepy taxi to go to the city centre. Download GRAB. It cost us 10$ to go to the city centre, 3 times cheaper than the price the taxi drivers offer.

Where to stay

Budget option: Old Quarter View Hanoi Hostel

Located in the heart of the Old Quarter, it is the perfect hostel for backpackers. Comfort and value at the same time, it has options for under 10$ a night, breakfast included.

Comfort option: Hanoi La Selva

Great facilities and lovely staff at a very reasonable price. Gorgeous rooms and located in the Old Quarter, next to Hoan Kiem lake.

Sapa -2 nights-

The landscapes of the very north of Vietnam are a must see and you shouldn’t miss them. The easiest place to visit is Sapa, this comes at a cost though. We’ve heard Sapa can be very touristic and a bit fake. However, if you’re clever and book the right trekking, your experience will have nothing to do with that.

TIP: You can avoid paying one night of accommodation by taking this very comfortable night bus.

Our Trekking

We booked a trekking called “Off the beaten track” with Sapa O’Chau and it was one of the best experiences we’ve had when travelling. More about this here and here.

Where to stay

Budget option: Sapa O’Chau

Sapa O’Chau is also a humble hotel. You can stay here the night before/after your trekking. The rooms are clean and quite cheap, so it has good value.

Comfort option: Eco Palms House Retreat

For those looking for a eco retreat. Bungalows in the middle of rice-fields with amazing views to the mountain. You can also appreciate the views while sipping wine in a bath tub.

Ha Long Bay – 1 or 2 nights-

Our 3 week Vietnam itinerary would not be complete without a visit to Ha Long Bay, one of the highlights of every visitor to Vietnam. A boat cruise will take you around the Bay for 1 or 2 nights, depending on how long you choose to stay. We’re budget travellers, so we chose 1 night. Most of the companies will include transportation from Hanoi to Ha Long Bay in the price.

We’ve written this post about how to choose between Sapa or Ha Long Bay in case you’re short on time or budget.

TIP: Boats usually spend the night in Ha Long Bay, meaning you end up sharing the night with hundreds of boats. Some cruises take a slightly different route, sleeping in Bai Tu Long Bay. This will make your experience MUCH BETTER. Our sunset was unforgettable.

Our choice

We took the package called “Off the beaten track” offered by Indochina Junk. With this option, you’ll spend the night in Bai Tu Long Bay. More about our experience here.

Ninh Binh -2 nights-

We got to Ninh Binh via train from Hanoi. We had a great time biking around Tam Coc, although we were a bit unlucky as we had heavy rain for one day, meaning we couldn’t make it to the national park. Still, we had an amazing time in this beautiful countryside area.

Must do things…

– Tam Coc lake tour. One hour stroll in a small boat. The landscape is very similar to Ha Long Bay but it is still worth it.
– Bich Dong Pagoda
– Bird Valley
– Mua cave. The cave is the least important thing, make sure you hike the mountain to get the perfect views!

Other things you could do…

– Cuc Phong National Park.

TIP: Rent a bike (most hostels provide very cheap bikes) and cycle everywhere. The roads are very safe and the views deserve to be appreciated. You won’t get the same experience using Tuk Tuks or motorbikes.

Where to stay

Budget option: Tam Coc Dream Hostel

Awesome value. The location is just perfect, very close to many restaurants and attractions. The woman who runs it is great and the family, in general, will do everything they can to make you happy. They also offer cheap bikes rental and will help you booking buses to your next destination.

Comfort option: Tam Coc Horizon Bungalow

Outstanding bungalows surrounded by amazing landscapes. The atmosphere and the staff are both simply incredible. Probably the best place to stay in Ninh Binh.

Hue -1 night-

We got to Hue by night bus from Ninh Binh. Be aware the bus wasn’t particularly safe, nothing do with the one we took to go to Sapa.

Hue is the city of the Citadel. While the Citadel is impressive and very interesting from a historical point of view, if we had to cut down one day from our itinerary it would be Hue. Still, we had a nice day and enjoyed the city. During the night there is a nice market by the river and many nice options for dinner.

Must do things…

– Hue Citadel and Forbidden City
– Walk stroll next to the river. There are many markets and restaurants

Where to stay

Budget option: Bonjour Hostel

Well located, very clean and offering rooms under 10$ the night depending on the dates. Delicious breakfast included! Just tremendous value.

Comfort option: The Scarlett Boutique Hotel

Very affordable luxury rooms (about 50$ the night). One of the most visited and prestigious hotels in Hue, you can’t go wrong with it.

Hoi An -6 nights-

Continuing our 3 week Vietnam itinerary, we’ll get to Hoi An. To get to this gem town, we strongly recommend the train if you come from Hue. It goes next to the Hai Van pass, a 21 km mountain pass with beautiful views to the sea.

Hoi An is no doubt our favourite city of Vietnam. Beach, rice fields and a beautiful old town make the city of the lanterns one of the most unique places we’ve ever visited. We have to admit we loved it so much we ended up staying for 10 days, 4 days more than we had planned.

Must do things…

– An Bang beach. Relaxing beach with very good and tasty restaurants. Also, you can do parasailing for 30$ 2 persons. We did it and was AMAZING.
– The old town at night. Have a stroll at the old town in the night, when all the lanterns are on. Simply beautiful.

– No better city to buy souvenirs. We bought a few too many lanterns (but we don’t regret it!).
– Ride a motorbike around Hoi An. We’ll never forget the joy of riding the motorbike from the city centre to the beach, passing next to the rice fields.

Other things you could do…

– Ancient town tour. Old houses, museums, Japanese covered bridge, etc.
– Marble Mountains, halfway between Da Nang and Hoi An.

TIP: Motorbike is a must in Hoi An. The roads are good and traffic is more “western” than in other places of the country. Another plus: you won’t have to deal with tuk-tuk or taxi drivers.

Where to stay

Budget option: Melody Boutique Villa Hoi An

Modern construction, clean, fresh and very comfortable. The staff is amazing and will help you with anything you need. Bicycles and scooters available for rental at very affordable prices. Amazing value (rooms under 15$ the night).

Comfort option: Little Town Villa

Luxury but affordable villa in the old town. Spacious rooms and tasteful decoration. The breakfast is great, with both local and international options. Swimming pool to cool down after a long walk around the old town.

Da Lat -3 nights-

Following our 3 week Vietnam itinerary, it is now time for some adventure! Da Lat is known for its hills, forests and waterfalls and it’s a place you can’t miss.

Must do things…

– Rapelling waterfalls
– Visiting Pongour Falls

Where to stay

Budget option: Gia Pham Hotel

Very comfortable private room for under 8$ per night. Value doesn’t get better than this. Extremely helpful staff and very good location.

Comfort option: Doc May Boutique Villa

A little boutique place to an exceptional price. The villa location is great and you can walk or cycle pretty much anywhere, including the night market.

Ho Chi Minh -2 nights-

The last stop of our 3 week Vietnam itinerary is Ho Chi Minh, also known as Saigon. We stayed 2 nights but 1 night-2 days works well too.

The capital of what it was “South Vietnam”, it is as vibrant or more than Hanoi. Also, it is a great city to learn more about Vietnam’s war with the Americans.

Must do things…

– Walk around District 1.
– Memorial war museum.
– Cu Chi Tunnels.

TIP: If you want to visit the real Cu Chi Tunnels, check out this post to visit them on your own.

Where to stay

Budget option: Couchsurfing with Saigon English Adventures

We did Couchsurfing with Saigon English Adventures English academy and absolutely loved it. You get to stay for free in exchange of being part of some English classes, where you will meet amazing locals. More about our experience here. Honestly, this was the nicest experience we’ve had in Vietnam.

Comfort option: Boholand Hostel

We didn’t stay here as we were Couchsurfing, but a couple of Canadian friends that we met did. Peaceful and well located in District one, close to all the main attractions. The woman who runs it apparently is very nice and the breakfast is great. Room prices are under 20$ per night

Other places you could visit

-Ha Giang. Not including this one in our 3 week Vietnam itinerary really hurt. This province is in the North of Vietnam, next to the Chinese border. This is a less touristic alternative to Sapa. Unfortunately, you should do it only if you have around 4-5 days, as it is very remote and takes some time to get there.

– Nha Trang. A coastal resort city in Southern Vietnam known for its beaches. From what we read, it is very touristic in a bad way (dirty beaches, party, etc).

– Phong Nha national park. Mountains, forest, rivers and caves. Good trekking and good adventures from our friends’ experiences!
